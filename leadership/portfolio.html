Areas of Improvement

Area 01

Inner Critic

My inner critic appears to be nothing but just a feeling. It can also be described as a void. It constantly puts me down and discourages me while giving me pessimistic thoughts. It unmotivates me and forces me to hide in a shell or behind a mask, which lowers my self-esteem. It takes a lot of effort for me to get out of the hole my inner critic has dug me.

I acknowledge that my inner critic is there and it may make me feel differently. So, I practice being aware of my feelings and this inner critic; I can catch it before it catches me. Once I know my feelings are being affected by my inner critic, I take a deep breath and remember my objectives. I tell myself the facts of what is going on and try my best to steer clear of the negative feelings my inner critic has planted in me.

Area 02

Fears

It takes courage to lead. Before having this courage, I, for one, am scared of a lot of things that come with leading. Such fears include stage fright, being the center of attention, and being wrong when my decision matters the most. These fears have controlled me for most of my life, but since the day I dared myself to go on the Kingda Ka, I have been a different person.

My courage challenge is to lead, one step at a time. I get around these fears by using the following concepts:
 (1) Do not take things personally
 (2) Don't make assumptions.
There are smaller detailed advice on more specific ways of getting over a fear, but these two steps have gotten me a long way this year and will continue to guide me as I venture into more unknown territory.
